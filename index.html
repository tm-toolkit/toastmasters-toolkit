Ah Counter

Tap filler words as each speaker talks

Opening Script
"Greetings Mr./Madam Toastmaster, fellow Toastmasters, and guests. The purpose of the Ah-Counter is to note words and sounds that are used as a "crutch" or "pause filler" by anyone who speaks. During the meeting, I will listen for overused words, including and, well, but, so, and you know. I will also listen for filler sounds, including ah, um, and er. I will also note when a speaker repeats a word or phrase, such as "I, I" or "This means, this means."

At the end of the meeting, I will report the number of times that each speaker used these expressions.

Thank you, Mr./Madam Toastmaster."

📺 How to set up OBS as your Zoom background click to expand ▾
📹 Video guide
▶ Watch on YouTube How to use OBS as a virtual camera in Zoom
1
Download & install OBS
Go to obsproject.com or search "OBS Studio" in the Microsoft Store. Install and open it. No special configuration needed at first launch.
2
Open the Display Window
Click 📺 Open Display Window above. A new browser window will open — this is the screen OBS will capture. Before continuing, enable the OBS Guide checkbox inside that window (top right corner) so you can see where to place your camera.
3
Add a Window Capture in OBS
In OBS, under Sources → click + → select Window Capture. In the dropdown, choose the browser window showing the display. Click OK. Right-click the source → Fit to screen so it fills the OBS canvas completely.
4
Add your camera on top
In OBS → Sources → + → Video Capture Device → select your webcam. Drag and resize it so it sits exactly over the "PLACE OBS CAMERA HERE" guide in the bottom-right corner.
5
Set OBS as your Zoom camera
In Zoom → Settings → Video → Camera → select OBS Virtual Camera. The timer display will now appear as your video background.
⚠
Disable auto-framing in Zoom
In Zoom → Settings → Video → uncheck "Auto-adjust my video" or "Auto-frame my video". If left on, Zoom will detect your face and zoom in, cutting off the timer display.
